[SECTION: ABOUT DST-CIMS]
The DST Centre for Interdisciplinary Mathematical Sciences (DST-CIMS) is a research and training centre at Banaras Hindu University (BHU), Varanasi, under the Institute of Science. It was established with funding from the Department of Science and Technology (DST), Government of India, vide letter number SR/S4/MS:428/07 dated 20 November 2007. The centre was created to improve the overall quality of research and education in the Mathematical Sciences in Uttar Pradesh and adjoining areas, by bringing the needed expertise and facilities to one convenient location. Its primary mission is to impart training and research in Mathematical Sciences and to establish core-group research facilities in the region.

[SECTION: COLLABORATING DEPARTMENTS]
DST-CIMS was formed with the active cooperation of the Departments of Mathematics, Statistics, Computer Science, and Applied Mathematics of BHU. Scientists from other departments, such as the Department of Physics, have also actively contributed to and participated in its activities. This interdisciplinary structure is the core strength of the centre.

[SECTION: COURSES OFFERED]
DST-CIMS offers postgraduate and doctoral programmes in the mathematical and computing sciences. The main postgraduate (M.Sc.) programmes associated with the centre include:
- M.Sc. in Statistics and Computing
- M.Sc. in Signal and Image Processing
The centre is also involved in Ph.D. (doctoral) research programmes across Mathematics, Statistics, Computer Science, and Applied Mathematics. For the exact current list of programmes, intake, and syllabi, please refer to the official DST-CIMS page on the BHU website or contact the centre directly.

[SECTION: ADMISSIONS AND ELIGIBILITY]
Admission to the M.Sc. programmes at BHU is generally done through the national-level entrance examination (CUET-PG) conducted for postgraduate admissions. Ph.D. admissions follow the BHU research admission process (such as RET / national fellowships as applicable). Eligibility typically requires a relevant bachelor's or master's degree with the required percentage as specified by BHU. Because eligibility criteria, cut-offs, and the admission calendar change every year, candidates should always verify the latest details from the official BHU admission portal (admission.bhu.ac.in) or the DST-CIMS office before applying.

[SECTION: FEES]
The fee structure for the M.Sc. and Ph.D. programmes at DST-CIMS is as per the standard fee norms of Banaras Hindu University (BHU) for the respective programme and academic session. Fees are paid through the official BHU online admission and registration system at the time of admission. The fees are generally modest as BHU is a central government university. Because the exact fee amounts are revised by the university every academic year, students should always check the latest fee details on the official BHU admission portal (admission.bhu.ac.in) or contact the DST-CIMS office. Eligible students may also reduce their costs through scholarships and fellowships (see the Scholarships and Fellowships section).

[SECTION: RESEARCH AREAS]
Research at DST-CIMS spans several areas of the mathematical sciences, including: Differential Geometry and Riemann-Finsler Geometry; General Relativity, Cosmology and Dynamical Systems; Statistics and Computing; Signal and Image Processing; Machine Learning; and Applications of Artificial Intelligence in Software Engineering. The centre supports interdisciplinary research drawing on Mathematics, Statistics, Computer Science, Applied Mathematics, and Physics.

[SECTION: ACADEMIC ACTIVITIES]
DST-CIMS regularly organises academic activities such as workshops, short-term training programmes, conferences, and seminars in the mathematical sciences. These activities are aimed at students, young researchers, and teachers. The Department of Statistics & DST-CIMS also conducts campus placements for its M.Sc. batches.

[SECTION: SCHOLARSHIPS AND FELLOWSHIPS]
Research scholars at DST-CIMS may be supported through standard fellowships available at BHU and national funding agencies (such as UGC/CSIR JRF/SRF and DST-supported project fellowships), subject to eligibility and availability. M.Sc. students may also be eligible for merit-based and need-based scholarships as per BHU norms. For exact scholarship and fellowship details, students should contact the DST-CIMS office or check the official BHU notifications.
